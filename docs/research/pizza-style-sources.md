# Pizza style source notes

Accessed: 2026-07-13

Purpose: research notes for the DoughTools Pizza Style Atlas. These notes support concise educational claims on `/styles`; they are not a recipe specification and they do not define DoughTools planner support.

## Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana — International Regulations / recipe guidance

- URL: https://www.pizzanapoletana.org/public/pdf/Disciplinare-2024-ENG.pdf
- URL: https://www.pizzanapoletana.org/en/ricetta_pizza_napoletana
- Source type: formal standard
- Factual points used:
  - Verace Pizza Napoletana is a regulated Neapolitan tradition with defined production characteristics.
  - AVPN guidance describes dough-ball weight and diameter ranges for the formal style.
  - The style is associated with hand stretching, restrained topping, high heat and a very fast bake.
- DoughTools use:
  - The Atlas distinguishes formal AVPN reference from home-pizza adaptation.
  - The page does not claim a home oven pizza is AVPN-compliant.

## Modernist Cuisine — Understanding Neapolitan Pizza

- URL: https://modernistcuisine.com/mc/understanding-neapolitan-pizza/
- Source type: technical expert guidance
- Factual points used:
  - Neapolitan pizza is recognized for a soft, airy, charred rim and short high-heat bake.
  - Heat, dough structure and topping restraint interact; the result is not controlled by one number alone.
- DoughTools use:
  - Supports the explanation of why a soft center and fast rim expansion depend on oven heat and handling.

## Serious Eats — New York-style pizza and pizza dough writing

- URL: https://www.seriouseats.com/new-york-style-pizza
- URL: https://www.seriouseats.com/the-pizza-lab-three-doughs-to-know
- Source type: technical expert guidance
- Factual points used:
  - New York pizza is slice-oriented, foldable and commonly uses low-moisture mozzarella.
  - Oil and sometimes sugar are practical dough adaptations for a longer, lower-temperature bake.
  - A Neapolitan-style dough can dry out during a much longer New York-style bake.
- DoughTools use:
  - Supports the distinction between Neapolitan and New York structure, cheese and bake profile.

## Buddy’s Pizza — Detroit-style origin story

- URL: https://buddyspizza.com/history/
- Source type: historical / origin source
- Factual points used:
  - Buddy’s traces Detroit-style pizza to 1946 and square pizza baked in forged-steel pans.
  - The pan is central to the light, crisp crust associated with the style.
- DoughTools use:
  - Supports the Detroit chapter’s pan-first explanation and product distinction from generic thick pizza.

## Pure Michigan — Detroit-style pizza history

- URL: https://www.michigan.org/article/trip-idea/history-of-detroit-style-pizza-where-to-find-it
- Source type: historical / regional guidance
- Factual points used:
  - Detroit-style pizza is strongly associated with deep steel pans, crisp exterior, airy square crust and caramelized cheese edge.
- DoughTools use:
  - Supports the Detroit chapter’s explanation of pan material, edge cheese and texture.

## Pizza Every Friday — Tonda-style pizza how-to

- URL: https://pizzaeveryfriday.substack.com/p/tonda-style-pizza-how-to
- Source type: practical expert guidance
- Factual points used:
  - Tonda means round; Roman tonda is thin, crisp and distinct from Neapolitan and al taglio.
  - Sauce and toppings often run close to the edge compared with a large Neapolitan cornicione.
- DoughTools use:
  - Supports the Roman Tonda distinction without presenting it as a formal universal regulation.

## Honest Cooking — Pizza al Taglio: Roman Style Pan Pizza

- URL: https://honestcooking.com/roman-style-pan-pizza-al-taglio/
- Source type: practical expert guidance
- Factual points used:
  - Pizza al taglio is a rectangular Roman pan pizza with a crisp bottom and airy interior.
  - High hydration is common in many formulas, but practical formulas vary.
- DoughTools use:
  - Supports the Roman al Taglio / Teglia chapter while avoiding one universal hydration claim.

## Adam Witt — Roman Pan Pizza / Pizza al Taglio
