## AVPN International Regulations

- Organization: Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana
- URL: https://www.pizzanapoletana.org/public/pdf/Disciplinare-2024-ENG.pdf
- Type: traditional regulation
- Points used:
  - True Neapolitan pizza has defined production characteristics.
  - Traditional Margherita and Marinara toppings are treated as part of the protected Neapolitan style.
  - Fast, high-heat Neapolitan baking changes how tomato moisture and topping balance should be understood.

## AVPN Pizza Napoletana recipe and preparation guide

- Organization: Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana
- URL: https://www.pizzanapoletana.org/en/ricetta_pizza_napoletana
- Type: traditional preparation guidance
- Points used:
  - Neapolitan pizza is baked as a fast, high-heat pizza with a raised cornicione.
  - Tomato topping is applied to the pizza rather than treated as a long-cooked pasta sauce.
  - Traditional practice distinguishes the pizza topping profile from a generic blended tomato sauce.

## AVPN “Marinara and her sisters”

- Organization: Associazione Verace Pizza Napoletana
- URL: https://www.pizzanapoletana.org/en/web_school/55-marinara_and_her_sisters
- Type: traditional/expert guidance
- Points used:
  - Marinara is built from tomato, oil, garlic and oregano.
  - Oregano is central to the Marinara profile.
  - Marinara is a simple historical tomato pizza, not a seafood sauce.

## Ooni AVPN Standard Pizza Marinara

- Organization/author: Ooni
- URL: https://ooni.com/blogs/recipes/avpn-standard-pizza-marinara
- Type: practical expert guidance
- Points used:
  - Practical Marinara examples use tomato, salt, thinly sliced garlic, oregano and extra-virgin olive oil.
  - Garlic quantity varies with clove size and personal preference.
  - Home cooks can use AVPN-inspired guidance without claiming every adaptation is AVPN-certified.

## USDA leftovers and food safety

- Organization: United States Department of Agriculture, Food Safety and Inspection Service
- URL: https://www.fsis.usda.gov/food-safety/safe-food-handling-and-preparation/food-safety-basics/leftovers-and-food-safety
- Type: food-safety guidance
- Points used:
  - General leftover guidance: refrigerate leftovers for 3–4 days or freeze for 3–4 months.
  - Refrigerate promptly, use safe handling, and discard food with unsafe handling history or spoilage signs.

## DoughTools practical interpretation

- Type: product guidance
- Points used:
