# Pizza oven and heat source notes

Accessed: 2026-07-13

Purpose: research notes for the DoughTools Oven and Heat Guide. These notes support educational oven and heat claims on `/ovens`; they do not define DoughTools planner options or change bake-profile logic.

## United States Fire Administration — Outdoor fire safety

- URL: https://www.usfa.fema.gov/prevention/outdoor-fires/
- Source category: formal safety guidance
- Factual points used:
  - Propane, charcoal and wood pellet grills should only be used outside.
  - Outdoor cooking equipment should be kept well away from siding, railings, eaves and overhanging branches.
  - Children and pets should be kept away from active outdoor cooking areas.
- DoughTools use:
  - The guide gives conservative safety reminders and directs users to their own equipment manual.

## National Fire Protection Association — Grilling safety

- URL: https://www.nfpa.org/education-and-research/home-fire-safety/grilling
- Source category: formal safety guidance
- Factual points used:
  - Keep children and pets away from the grilling area.
  - Use fuel and starter products only as intended.

## King Arthur Baking — Cooking pizza on a baking steel

- URL: https://www.kingarthurbaking.com/blog/2016/12/03/baking-steel-pizza-dough
- Source category: expert practical advice
- Factual points used:
  - Steel conducts heat efficiently and can cook pizza crust quickly in a home oven.
  - Steel is useful for home-oven pizza but still requires preheating and method control.
- DoughTools use:
  - Supports the plain-language steel explanation: a thick surface stores heat; steel releases heat quickly.

## Serious Eats — Baking steel and pizza stone testing
